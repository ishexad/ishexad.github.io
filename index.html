About

Data with
a point of view.

I'm a data analyst building a practice around data journalism — using numbers to illuminate the structural forces that shape our world. My work bridges the gap between statistical rigour and narrative clarity.

Based in the UK, I'm particularly drawn to questions where the data tells a more complex story than the headline suggests — like how between-country income convergence can coexist with rising inequality within nations.

Current focus areas

Within-country vs between-country inequality trends
Democratic governance and institutional resilience
China, India, and Sub-Saharan Africa as comparative cases
Translating statistics into compelling social media narratives
Global power imbalances in international institutions

Inequality · Economics

The Great Divergence Within

An analysis of how GDP convergence between nations masks a simultaneous rise in internal inequality — examining China, India, and Sub-Saharan Africa through World Bank and SWIID datasets.

Democracy · Governance

Democracy Index Dashboard

Interactive dashboard tracking democratic backsliding across 167 countries using V-Dem and EIU data. Highlights correlations between electoral integrity and press freedom scores over the past two decades.

Python Plotly V-Dem API Streamlit

Global Power · Institutions

Who Runs the IMF?

A data-driven investigation into voting power distribution at the IMF and World Bank, revealing how quota systems entrench historical power imbalances and disadvantage emerging economies in crisis negotiations.
